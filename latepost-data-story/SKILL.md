# LatePost-Inspired Data Story

Use this skill for short Chinese business stories built around one dataset, chart, or historical comparison. The goal is compact interpretation, not comprehensive analysis. Do not reproduce LatePost text or claim a chart was compiled unless the user supplied the data.

Read [source-notes.md](references/source-notes.md) for source examples and observed formats.

## Data Gate

Before drafting, verify:

- Dataset source, date range, unit, geography, and denominator.
- Whether values are nominal, real, cumulative, annualized, adjusted, or estimated.
- What the chart actually proves and what it cannot prove.

If data is missing, produce a data request/checklist instead of inventing numbers.

## Article Shape

Target 500-1200 Chinese characters.

1. Start with a historical anchor, recent quote, or striking comparison.
2. Say what data was gathered and why that lens matters.
3. Explain the main pattern in two or three short paragraphs.
4. Add one exception or caveat.
5. Close with the broader shift the data reveals.

Use no subheads unless the user asks for a chart package with multiple panels.

## Style Moves

- Prefer concise paragraphs of 1-3 sentences.
- Use the number, then the meaning. Avoid isolated statistics.
- Connect eras: "曾经...", "多年后...", "现在..."
- Make the title plain and searchable: `[现象]丨晚点小数据`.
- Let the final sentence reframe the chart rather than summarize it.

## Chart Caption Pattern

Use captions that contain:

- what is measured
- time range
- source
- one sentence of interpretation

Example structure: `图：[指标]（[年份]-[年份]）。来源：[来源]。最大的变化不是 [表面变化]，而是 [结构变化]。`

## Avoid

- Do not overfit a grand theory to a thin dataset.
- Do not mix incomparable units.
- Do not imply causation from correlation.
- Do not use vague phrases like "数据说明一切".
- Do not copy LatePost sentences or chart captions.
