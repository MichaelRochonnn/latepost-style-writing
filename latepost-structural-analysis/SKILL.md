# LatePost-Inspired Structural Analysis

Use this skill for analytical Chinese business essays that explain a company's hidden constraints, not just its headline performance. It is especially suited to financial reports, industry chains, AI infrastructure, energy, autos, platforms, and capital markets. Do not provide investment advice or copy LatePost wording.

Read [source-notes.md](references/source-notes.md) for observed examples.

## Evidence Standard

For current or financial claims, browse and use primary sources whenever possible: annual reports, earnings releases, transcripts, prospectuses, exchange filings, regulator documents, and company announcements. Cite dates clearly. Separate facts from inference.

## Analytical Frame

Find the structural contradiction:

- high margin versus high reinvestment
- technical lead versus customer dependence
- growth certainty versus revenue-recognition risk
- global opportunity versus geopolitical or regional concentration
- ecosystem control versus partner economics
- scale advantage versus weak free cash flow

The thesis should name both sides of the contradiction.

## Article Shape

Target 3000-7000 Chinese characters.

1. Opening: use the latest result, market reaction, or data anomaly.
2. Thesis: state the contradiction and why it matters.
3. Mechanism 1: explain the business model or accounting logic.
4. Mechanism 2: explain how that logic creates risk or fragility.
5. Mechanism 3: show the industry-chain or customer-power dimension.
6. Counterweight: identify the real strengths that still matter.
7. Conclusion: explain the condition under which the thesis changes.

Subheads should be argumentative, e.g. `技术领先，结构脆弱`, not merely descriptive.

## Language Pattern

- Use causal chains: "这意味着...", "进一步带来...", "一旦...就会..."
- Define terms briefly when they carry the argument, such as DBT, HBM, ASP, or free cash flow.
- Make accounting and industry logic readable without dumbing it down.
- Use "可能", "表明", "更像是" when moving from evidence to inference.
- Keep the tone cool. The drama should come from the structure.

## Risk Controls

- Avoid stock recommendations, target prices, or trading calls.
- Do not treat management guidance as fact.
- Do not use unsourced market estimates as hard truth.
- Mention uncertainty when estimates are derived.
- If the user asks for publishable work, include a source checklist before final drafting.

## Avoid

- Do not turn the article into a bullish/bearish memo.
- Do not bury the thesis under definitions.
- Do not overstate one quarter of data as destiny.
- Do not copy LatePost passages; use high-level editorial mechanics only.
