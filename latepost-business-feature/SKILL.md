# LatePost-Inspired Business Feature

Use this skill for Chinese long-form business features that explain how a company, product, or industry reached a turning point. Borrow high-level editorial patterns from LatePost-style reporting; do not copy article language or present the piece as LatePost.

Read [source-notes.md](references/source-notes.md) when you need calibrated sample structures.

## Core Thesis

Build the article around a tension, not a topic:

- fast growth versus fragile economics
- old capability versus new market rules
- product success versus brand uncertainty
- technical lead versus commercial dependence
- global ambition versus local constraint

State the tension early, then let each section test it from a different angle.

## Article Shape

Target 2500-6000 Chinese characters.

1. Opening pressure: begin with a recent event, data point, product launch, policy change, or market shock.
2. Thesis turn: define what the event reveals about the company or industry.
3. First mechanism: explain the business model, organization, supply chain, or product logic.
4. Second mechanism: show why the obvious answer is insufficient.
5. Human/organizational layer: introduce managers, engineers, users, partners, or internal incentives when sourced.
6. Tradeoff section: identify what the company must give up to win.
7. Ending: close on a still-open problem; avoid triumphant certainty.

Use 3-5 subheads. Good subheads contain a judgment or paradox, not just a noun.

## Paragraph Rhythm

- Start with short declarative paragraphs, then widen into explanation.
- Alternate evidence paragraphs and interpretation paragraphs.
- Use numbers as load-bearing beams, not decoration.
- Put transitions in plain language: "难点也随之出现", "这解释了一个矛盾面", "从结果看".
- Let quotes complicate the argument. Do not quote for color only.

## Title Patterns

- `[公司/产品]转型第 N 年：[动作/矛盾]`
- `从 [A] 到 [B]：[公司] 的 [现实约束/核心韧性]`
- `[品类]的[时间跨度]：[巨头试错/后来者挑战/新一轮竞争]`
- `[行业变化]， [市场格局]继续强化`

## Fact Discipline

For current claims, browse or use primary sources before drafting. For financial, legal, medical, or investment-sensitive claims, use primary filings, company announcements, transcripts, or regulator documents where possible and avoid advice.

## Avoid

- Do not write a marketing case study.
- Do not overuse metaphors or polished one-liners.
- Do not make every company decision sound inevitable.
- Do not hide weak sourcing behind confident prose.
- Do not imitate exact LatePost phrasing; use the structural method only.
