# LatePost-Inspired Dialogue Profile

Use this skill to shape interviews and profiles around a person's decisions, contradictions, and operating model. It supports both Q&A editing and narrative interview features. Do not fabricate quotes or mimic exact LatePost wording.

Read [source-notes.md](references/source-notes.md) when sample calibration is useful.

## Source Rule

If the user has not provided a transcript, notes, recording summary, or quotes, draft an interview plan or profile outline instead of a finished Q&A. Mark any proposed answer framing as "待采访验证".

## Profile Shape

1. Open with why this person matters now.
2. Give one concise biographical arc, emphasizing decisions and constraints.
3. Identify the central question the interview tests.
4. Move into dialogue with questions that pressure-test the thesis.
5. Close with the person's unresolved problem, not a personality compliment.

## Q&A Editing

- Keep `晚点：` and speaker labels only when the user wants a LatePost-like Q&A format.
- Make questions short, specific, and mildly adversarial.
- Ask about moments, tradeoffs, and costs: "当时为什么不...", "代价是什么", "你后来怎么修正".
- Preserve the interviewee's logic and speech texture; remove filler and repeated setup.
- Insert brief narrative bridges only when they clarify time, place, or stakes.

## Question Bank

Use these patterns:

- "你什么时候意识到原来的判断错了？"
- "这个决定最反直觉的地方是什么？"
- "当所有人都在做 X，为什么你选择 Y？"
- "你怎么判断这是长期变化，不只是一个周期？"
- "组织里谁会反对？你怎么说服他们？"
- "如果重来一次，哪件事不会再做？"

## Title Patterns

- `晚点对话[人物]：[一句人物自我认知或核心矛盾]`
- `独家对话[公司/人物]：我们不想[动作]，但不能不[动作]`
- `[人物]：为了做一个真正的 [角色]，我付出了什么`

## Avoid

- Do not flatter the interviewee by default.
- Do not turn the piece into biography unless the biography explains a current decision.
- Do not invent direct speech.
- Do not make every answer sound more concise, strategic, or polished than the source supports.
- Do not copy LatePost passages or dialogue turns.
